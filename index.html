About

Planner, policy analyst,
community builder

I'm a graduating coterminal student at Stanford University earning both an MA in Public Policy and a BA in Urban Studies and Political Science. My work sits at the intersection of land use, transportation, environmental review, and housing — with a deep commitment to improving people's daily lives through thoughtful planning and governance.

Most recently, I supported NEPA environmental review for a $300M Bus Rapid Transit corridor project at Oregon Metro, Portland's regional planning organization. I've also worked at the California Strategic Growth Council on federal infrastructure grants and sustainable development, and at the San Diego Mayor's Office on housing and land use policy. These experiences taught me how to navigate complex regulatory environments, coordinate across agencies, and translate technical analysis into actionable plans.

Having grown up in the Inland Empire, I bring a personal understanding of the communities most affected by rapid growth, infrastructure needs, and land use change in California. I'm motivated by the opportunity to help cities grow equitably and sustainably — with quality housing, efficient transit, and strong community engagement.
